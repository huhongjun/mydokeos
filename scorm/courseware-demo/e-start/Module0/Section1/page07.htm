When you are sick there are a number of procedures that must be followed in accordance with company policy.

Employees are required to contact their manager before 10.00am on a daily basis if they will be absent from work due to sickness or injury.

The Company expectation is that no employee should be absent for more than 7 working days in any one year due to sickness related problems.

Click on the flashing dice to continue...

If an employee is away from work for more than 5 working days a medical certificate issued by a doctor will be necessary in addition to the Self-Certification Sick leave form. For each additional time period away due to sickness or injury a separate medical certificate will be required.

Click on the flashing dice to continue...

Upon returning, we ask all staff to report to their direct line manager before commencing work, we reserve the right to carry out a return to work interview.

At this meeting the employee and the line manager may complete a 'Return to work meeting' form agreeing the reason for the absence, the period of absence and, where appropriate, what course of action is required as a result of the absence. If appropriate both parties must sign this form before forwarding it to Human Resources.

Click on the flashing dice to continue...

Provided that you meet the qualifying criteria, you will be entitled to Statutory Sick Pay for that time you are absent through illness for a period of up to 28 weeks in any period of illness.

Please note that this payment is for tax reasons treated as wages in that it is subject to PAYE and National Insurance.
