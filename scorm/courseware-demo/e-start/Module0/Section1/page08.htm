    • Jury Service

    If you are required to serve on jury duty or a similar activity you should advise your direct line manager as soon as possible in writing.
    During this time you will receive an amount equivalent to your adjusted gross salary, taking into account any compensation you may receive directly for performing this service.
    In some circumstances it may not be possible for you to attend jury duty due to client or business needs. If this is the case, you should speak to a member of the Human Resources department who will write to support an application for jury service to be deferred.
    Maternity Leave

    Please contact HR for further details on the Company Maternity policy.
    Paternity Leave

    In line with the company's maternity leave policy; employees who become fathers are permitted to take 5 days paternity leave with no service qualifications necessary. In the case of multiple births 5 days is still the maximum paid paternity leave offered Paternity leave should be taken within the first two months of the baby's birth or adoption. As with maternity leave, new fathers will need to provide a medical certificate confirming the birth or details of adoption.
    Parental Leave

    In line with the Parental Leave Directive, all employees with more than one year's service have the right to take up to three months unpaid leave within the first five years of the birth or adoption of a child. This applies to parents of children born after 15th December 1999 only. Employees who wish to request Parental Leave should discuss where possible, appropriate time periods to ensure client and business needs will continue to be met.

    If you require further information in relation to Parental leave please contact a member of Human Resources.
