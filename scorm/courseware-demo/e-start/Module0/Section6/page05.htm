The law actually protects a wide range of people against unfair treatment at work. The following areas are protected by discrimination law:

  1. Race
  2. Religion
  3. Gender
  4. Sexual Orientation
  5. Disability
  6. Age

Race

The Race Relations Act of 1976 makes it illegal to treat anyone less favourably on the basis of their race, nationality, ethnic origin or colour.

Religion

The laws around religion and belief only came into force in 2003 but now protect people from being discriminated against.

Gender

The Sex Discrimination Act of 1975 makes it illegal to treat anyone less favourably because on the basis of their gender. The Equal Pay Act of 1970 also gives men and women the right to equal treatment and equal pay when carrying out equivalent jobs.

Sexual Orientation

Under Employment Equality Regulations of 2003, it's illegal to treat anyone less favourably because their heterosexual, gay, lesbian or bi-sexual.

Disability

The Disability Discrimination Act 1995 makes it illegal to treat anyone less favourably on the grounds of disability which is defined as "A physical or mental impairment which has substantial and long term adverse effects on a persons' ability to carry out normal activities.

Age

Finally, under the Employment Equality Regulations of 2006, it's illegal to treat anyone less favourably on the basis of their age. This could take many forms, including turning someone down for a job, overlooking them for promotion, or denying them access to training.
