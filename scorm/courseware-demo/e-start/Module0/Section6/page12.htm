ExampleCompany recognises and rewards loyal service from its employees.
If you are leaving the company there are some procedures to follow:
  • Termination of Employment
  • Unused Annual Leave
  • Exit Interviews
  • Reference for former employees
  • Company Benefits
    • Termination of Employment

    Should you wish to leave the company, you are normally required to give notice of this event in writing.

    Conversely in the case of redundancy, you will receive a letter of termination with detailing an equivalent notice period.

    The period of notice is specified in your contract of employment. The amount of notice can be increased by mutual consent for various reasons without infringing on legal requirements.

    Unused Annual Leave

    If the employee is entitled to holiday at the time of offering their notice, a maximum of 1 week holiday may be take during this period.

    If the holiday entitlement is not taken, payment will be made in lieu of such entitlement.

    Exit Interviews

    HR will conduct an exit interview before the employee leaves the Company. This interaction is designed for us to receive feedback on our performance as an employer.

    This interview is totally confidential will not effect any references.

    References

    Verification of employment (dates, job titles, salary etc.) can be referred to the HR Department.

    Character references can be organized with your current line manager.

    Company Benefits

    All employee benefit schemes terminates at the end of the notice period.
