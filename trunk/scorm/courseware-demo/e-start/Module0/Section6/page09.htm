The following procedures and policies have been developed in order to minimise breaches of confidentiality and abuse of company property and time. It is essential all employees are aware of the guidelines.

Intranet

The Intranet has been developed to encourage communication throughout ExampleCompany. It is an excellent medium for keeping colleagues up to date with departmental progress and individual achievements. We encourage staff to utilise the Intranet to the full. New starters should go into the Intranet to familiarise themselves with colleagues, ExampleCompany's background and recent developments. Should you feel that something could be incorporated into the Intranet or would like something amended you should contact the Marketing department.

Email/Internet

The internet and email should not be used for non-worked related purposes. Such use could subject an employee to disciplinary procedures.

Examples of activities include:
  • An attempt to create a sexually or racially hostile work environment;
  • Sending hate mail, make discriminatory or libellous remarks or other malicious uses;
  • Send, view, download or forward abusive, threatening, obscene or harassing material;
  • Solicit outside business ventures, advertise for personal enterprises, promote political causes..
    • Telephones

    The company recognizes that there may occasionally be times when personal calls must be made or received during business hours. Such calls must be held to a minimum, however, and must not interfere with the employee's work. Employees are encouraged to make such calls during their breaks or at lunchtime.

    When a long-distance call results in a charge, the call must be billed to the caller's home phone number or charges reimbursed to the company.

    Mobile Phones

    Use of personal mobile phones must be held to a minimum and must not interfere with the employee's work. Employees are encouraged to make personal calls during their breaks or at lunchtime.
