The disciplinary procedure is a fair and open system which ensures that all employees are treated equally. In proportion to the seriousness of the case, the procedure may be started at any of the stages outlined below
Verbal warning

After the case has been reviewed with the employee and line manager, the Company may consider that there is no need to proceed with a formal procedure. In such cases an informal verbal warning is given.

The date and the reason for the verbal warning will be documented. This will be removed from the personnel file after the period of 6 months.

Written warning

If the misdemeanor is categorized as one of serious misconduct then a formal written warning will be issued. The date and the reason for the written warning will be documented and held within the employee’s personnel file for a period of 1 year.

Steps will then be taken to review the employee’s behaviour.

Final Written Warning

If the offence is repeated or it is very serious then a final written warning may be issued..

The employee will be informed upon receiving the warning that the next step in the procedure will be dismissal. A copy of the final written warning will be sent to Human Resources and kept on file for a period of 1 year.

Appeal procedure

An employee can appeal against the findings of the disciplinary panel. Appeals may be made on the grounds that the employee is not guilty of the misdemeanor or if the punishment is not proportionate to the offence.

Appeals should be made to the HR department.. The appeal should take the form of a letter, outlining the grounds for appeal and should be lodged within 5 days of receipt of the formal letter.

The appeal will take place within 5 days of receipt of the appeal letter. The appeal should be heard by a manager who has been previously uninvolved in the case.
