This section demonstrates classic signs of sexual harassment, press [More...] for examples of how not to behave:
The Company is committed to a working environment free from harassment and intimidation. Harassment under the Sex Discrimination Act 1975 and is not tolerated.
Physical harassment - this involves unsolicited physical conduct ranging from patting, pinching or unnecessarily touching another employee's body;
Verbal harassment - includes offensive language, sexually orientated jokes and comments. or repeated unwelcome invitations.
Non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature - includes the display pictures, objects or written materials which display men and woman as sexual objects; also includes the leering, whistling or making crude gestures.
Threatening harassment – this harassment usually occurs between a manager and a subordinate. A reward or sexual favour is demanded or implied, and a failure to comply results in negative consequences for the subordinate.
Every employee at ExampleCompany is responsible for identifying the behaviour outlined in order to maintain a harassment-free working environment. In particular if you are responsible for other employees you must ensure that:
  • You are aware of, and understand the company's policy in relation to sexual harassment and equal opportunities;
  • Your office is free of visual harassment;
  • You tackle physical and verbal harassment as soon as it is identified;
  • You are sympathetic and supportive of any individual who states that they have been harassed;
  • You treat sexual harassment cases with complete confidentiality;
  • You ensure that the harassment is immediately stopped..
