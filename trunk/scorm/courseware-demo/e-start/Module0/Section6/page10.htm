Gross Misconduct

ExampleCompany will terminate the employment of any employee who is found guilty of gross misconduct. Anyone suspected of such an offence will be suspended without pay pending investigation. Examples of gross misconduct include:
  • Deliberate damage to property;
  • Threats of physical violence;
  • Fraud;
  • Gross Insubordination;
  • Working under the influence of alcohol or drugs;
  • Breaking the law;
  • Disclosing company secrets to competitors.
  • ExampleCompany will ensure that any employee is prosecuted if their has been a clear case of criminal activity.
Serious Misconduct

The following are examples offences which would normally result in a final warning or instant dismissal:
  • Persistent minor misconduct
  • Rudeness to clients
  • Damage to property
  • Abusive language or obscene behaviour
  • Failure to comply with safety rules
  • Bullying or discrimination of any kind
Minor Misconduct

Such offenses usually result in a verbal or written warning. Examples include:
  • Repeated poor standards
  • Poor time-keeping
  • Prolonged absence from work
  • Abuse of power over subordinates
