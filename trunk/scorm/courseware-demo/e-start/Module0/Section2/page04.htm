Petrol Allowances
In most cases the cost of petrol for business purposes will be reimbursed subject to the provision of satisfactory expense claims. Current petrol allowance for vehicles with engines up to 1,000cc is 40p, greater than 1,500cc is 45p and over 2,000cc is 63p.
Hotels, conferences etc.
The local travel agency will, via your travel administrator, source hotels, venues for conferences and large meetings. If possible, only hotels should be used where special deals have been negotiated.

A single room in a moderately priced business class hotel, usually a 3 star hotel, is the standard.

Combined business and personal travel
When an employee combines business and personal travel, the costs of the personal travel (supplementary hotel, travel and meals) will not be reimbursed by ExampleCompany.
Business Meals and Meetings
Personal Meals while Travelling (Subsistence) ExampleCompany will reimburse employees for the actual cost of meals incurred while travelling on company business. The expense for personal meals while travelling should be reasonable in relation to the location of travel, but should not exceed £40 or equivalent per day. Whilst this is the maximum that an employee may spend, only actual costs as evidenced by receipts will be reimbursed. Note: An evening meal may only be claimed if the employee is staying overnight.
Business meals
Business meals are all meals taken with clients, prospects or business associates during which a business discussion takes place. Business meals are reimbursable when they are directly related to or associated with the active conduct of business. Occasionally it may be appropriate to hold meetings off site. Expenditure should be limited to £20.00 per head for staff or £50.00, including drinks, per head for clients.

To comply with tax regulations the following information must be provided on employee travel and expense reports (or the attached original receipts) for all business meals and entertainment expenses:

  • name, title and company of all attendees
  • name and location of establishment where the event took place
  • amount and date of expense
  • specific business topic discussed.
    • Telephone Usage
    ExampleCompany will pay for local and long distance business calls made outside of your normal office location provided the calls are substantiated with a detailed bill.
    Personal Calls

    ExampleCompany will reimburse charges for reasonable personal long distance calls while travelling on business provided the calls are substantiated with a detailed bill.
    Telephone calls to home when travelling on business are reimbursable provided they are of a duration no longer than 10 minutes per day.

    Reimbursable/Non-reimbursable Expenses
    Some expenses, even when incurred in connection with company business will not be reimbursable. The following two lists are indicative of the types of business-related expenses which will be considered reimbursable or non-reimbursable respectively. The lists are not exhaustive.

    Other Reimbursable Expenses

  • Tips for porters, food services, etc
  • Parking
  • Passport or visa
  • Laundry required while travelling on business for 5 days or more
  • Meetings, seminars, conferences, training courses
  • Dues for memberships in trade, professional and technical organisations which are directly related to the employee's duties

    • Other Non-Reimbursable Expenses
  • Personal entertainment: in-flight movies, hotel room movies, sporting events, green fees, etc
  • Personal articles: clothing, toiletries, barber/hairdresser fees, sweets, etc.
  • Parking tickets
    • Payments made in advance
    Where it is anticipated that large incidental costs will be experienced, a request for an advance can be made. It must be received at least 5 working days prior to the date required. An Expense Claim Form must be completed to show how this advance was spent and submitted as soon as the employee returns from the trip. Failure to submit an Expense Claim Form before the second payroll run following the advance will result in the amount being reclaimed through the employee's salary. The Finance and Admin Department have the facility to assist in the event of a travel emergency requiring immediate payment. The contacts during office hours are the UK Office Manager the UK Receptionist. The out of hour's contact is the Finance Director.
