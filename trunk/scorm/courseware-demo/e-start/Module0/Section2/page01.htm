Authorisation to Travel

Prior to committing to a travel booking, the traveller must obtain authorisation, using the Travel Request Form, from his/her line manager or cost centre manager, failing this by the Finance Director. Travel requests should be made at least one week in advance, wherever possible.

All completed and authorised forms should be passed to your local travel administrator.

Any unauthorised travel may result in disciplinary action.

Purchasing Tickets

  • All air travel should be booked using the least expensive class of service. The traveller should always use the lowest applicable airfare and wait-list for those less expensive fares when they are not immediately available.
  • All flights must be booked via your local travel administrator so that complete travel expenditure reporting can be provided.
  • If possible, please consider starting your meetings a few minutes later or earlier to take advantage of the lower cost or negotiated discounts offered by carriers.
  • Wherever possible, flights should be booked well in advance and restricted fares used where practical. Ensure you are aware of penalties and non-refundable costs associated with advance bookings and restricted fares. Each employee should assess the risk of change to travel plans when booking flights and should seek approval of their line manager/cost centre manager when in doubt.

    • Foreign Currency

    Cash expenditure should be converted into the local currency using the exchange rate obtained when buying the currency. The receipt for the conversion must be attached to the expense report. Please note that the conversion fee charged can also be claimed as a business expense.

    Weekend Travel

    Employees needing air travel departing on Sunday or returning on Friday may include a Saturday night stay in their travel plans in order to take advantage of cheaper fares. The employee must obtain written approval from their respective line manager/cost centre manager for weekend travel prior to the purchase of the ticket. Approval will be based upon individual circumstances and demonstrated savings to ExampleCompany. In addition, the employee must obtain a documented lowest fare routing vs. weekend fare comparison, which will serve as verification of savings against anticipated expenses.

    This fare comparison must be submitted when obtaining approval for weekend travel. ExampleCompany will reimburse the employee's actual lodging, meals and ground transportation for the personal portion of the trip but only to the extent of the difference between the lowest cost fare and the weekend fare and only to the extent such expenses are normally reimbursable under this policy.

    Class of Travel

  • Economy class travel only is allowed to all European destinations. However, travel in Business Class may be allowed, provided a special route deal includes an upgrade to Business Class without surcharge.
  • Business class travel is permitted to locations where the journey consists of 6 hours or more in any one flying sector.

    • Travel Changes/Cancellations

    Changes to travel plans should be made with your travel administrator immediately.

    Changes that need to be made out-of-hours can be done directly with the local supplier. Please see your travel administrator for the contact numbers prior to your travel date.

    Unused or cancelled issued tickets must be returned to your travel administrator for refund within two weeks of the planned commencement of the trip.

    Travel Limitations on Same Flight

    To the extent it is practical to do so by advanced planning, commercial airline travel should be scheduled so that:

  • no more than two members of the EMT/Board travel together on any one flight
  • no more than two directors travel on the same flight
  • a concentration on one flight of too many employees within the same location or function is avoided.
