Written Assessments
The process of hazard identification, risk quantification and risk controls are recorded in a written assessment.

On this form you should be able to identify:

  1. What are the risks involved in each task?
  2. What could result if the unexpected happened?
  3. What is required to ensure safety?

In the normal course of events any assessment should be reviewed every 12 months. It should be signed at the time of issue. At this stage it is then a live document and can be used in any subsequent court case either as evidence for the prosecution or as evidence for the defence. It must not be shoved into a drawer and ignored.
